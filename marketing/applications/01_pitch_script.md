# CodesRock: UNICEF Startup Lab 6-Minute Pitch

**Format:** 6 Minutes Total
**Speakers:** Triumph (CEO/Tech Lead) & Ellen (COO/Education Lead)
**Pacing:** ~130 words per minute. Practice this with a timer to ensure you don't get cut off.

---

## 1. The Hook & The Problem (1 Min) - TRIUMPH
**Triumph:** Good morning panel. I’m Triumph, and this is my co-founder, Ellen. We are the team behind **CodesRock**.

The World Health Organization explicitly warns against screen time for children under 5. Yet, we know that computational thinking—logic, sequencing, and problem-solving—is the foundational language of the 21st century.

So, how do we teach a 4-year-old the logic of a software engineer without handing them a tablet? 

Currently, Ghanaian preschools either ignore STEM entirely, or they expose very young children to excessive screen time that harms cognitive development.

**CodesRock** bridges this gap. We are Ghana's premier screen-free computational thinking program. We use physical robots, tactile flashcards, and culturally relevant activity books to teach "unplugged" coding through pure, active play.

## 3. Business Model & Traction (1.5 Mins) - TRIUMPH
**Triumph:** We operate a B2B2C model with two core revenue streams:

1. **B2B (Schools):** A turnkey solution. Schools pay a starting investment of ₵5,850 for the Classroom Robot Kit, training materials, and curriculum access. We then charge a recurring fee for ongoing support and gamified portal access.

## 4. UNICEF SDGs & Impact (1 Min) - ELLEN
**Ellen:** CodesRock is proudly mission-driven, directly addressing three UN Sustainable Development Goals:

- **SDG 4 (Quality Education):** We are introducing world-class STEM skills to early learners, historically a neglected demographic in African tech education. Crucially, 68% of our outreach targets low-income demographics, ensuring no child is left behind.
- **SDG 5 (Gender Equality):** By introducing tech without the intimidated "screen" factor, we are closing the confidence gap for young girls before it even starts.
- **SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure):** We are building a pipeline of logical thinkers who will become Africa's future innovators.

## 5. Needs, Challenges & Future Plans (1 Min) - TRIUMPH
**Triumph:** But scaling hardware in Africa is hard.
Our **biggest business challenge** is supply chain logistics and the capital required to bulk-manufacture our physical kits to drive down unit costs for lower-income schools. 
Our **biggest technology challenge** is scaling `app.codesrock.com`—our gamified teacher tracking portal—to handle hundreds of schools reliably, offline and online.

**Our Roadmap:**
- **6 Months:** Onboard 50 new partner schools for the 2026 academic year.
- **12 Months:** Localize our physical curriculum into two additional Ghanaian languages.
- **24 Months:** Regional expansion across West Africa (Nigeria & Côte d'Ivoire).
