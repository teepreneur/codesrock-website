## Part 2: Panel Q&A Preparation (12 Minutes)

*The panel (MEST Africa, UNICEF Ghana, KOICA Ghana) will ask tough questions. Use the **STAR method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) when answering.*

### Expected Question 1: Unit Economics & Scalability
**Panel:** *"Hardware is expensive. How do you plan to scale this to low-income schools across Ghana?"*
**Triumph:** 
- **Acknowledge:** It is our primary challenge. 
- **Action:** Currently, our kits are manufactured at a small scale. However, the core of our IP is the *curriculum and the pedagogy*, not just the plastic robot. 
- **Future:** With UNICEF/KOICA's support, we want to optimize our supply chain for bulk manufacturing, driving the unit cost down so we can offer subsidized tiers to low-income public schools while maintaining margins on premium private schools and the B2C parent market.

### Expected Question 2: Competition
**Panel:** *"How is this different from existing STEM toys like Cubetto or Lego Education?"*
**Ellen:**
- **Differentiation 1:** Price point. Western kits are priced out of the African market. 
- **Differentiation 2:** Cultural Relevance. Our activity books and songs are developed specifically for the Ghanaian context. We aren't just importing a toy; we've built a localized curriculum.
- **Differentiation 3:** The Teacher Portal. We solve for the lack of IT-trained teachers in preschools, which imported toys do not address.

### Expected Question 3: Measuring SDG Impact
**Panel:** *"You mentioned SDG 4 and SDG 5. How are you actually measuring the 'Quality' of this education?"*
**Ellen:**
- **Metrics:** We don't just measure 'kits distributed'. We use a baseline assessment tool before a school implements CodesRock, and a follow-up assessment 3 months later.
- **Result:** This is how we validated our +200% improvement in collaborative problem-solving. Furthermore, because our kits are screen-free, we see equal engagement rates between young boys and girls, establishing tech confidence early to address SDG 5.

### Expected Question 4: The 24-Month Future
**Panel:** *"Where is CodesRock in 2 years, and how does this Lab help you get there?"*
**Triumph:**
- **Vision:** In 24 months, we are the standard early-years STEM curriculum in West Africa. We will have localized into multiple languages and have 200+ active partner schools.
- **The Ask:** We need MEST's network for strategic scale, and UNICEF/KOICA's deep understanding of educational deployment to help us transition from a B2B private school model to a model that can penetrate the public education sector.
