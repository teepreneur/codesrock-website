## 📋 Part 1: Exercise Submission – Design Your Revenue Model + Forecast

### A. Primary Revenue Model Selection & Justification
We implement a **Hybrid Subscription & Materials Replenishment** revenue model, anchored in our B2B2C school partnership program.
- **Why this model fits:**
  - **School Budgets:** Upfront capital expenditure is minimized through a tiered onboarding fee (GHS 5,850 for private, GHS 3,600 sponsored for public), which includes the hardware (Rockbots and Logic Cards) and training.
  - **Predictable Cash Flow:** Monthly portal subscriptions starting Term 2 (GHS 50/teacher + GHS 5/student) provide predictable recurring revenue to fund continuous portal feature updates, server hosting, and high-touch school success managers.
  - **High-Margin Material Sales:** Local printing of student activity books (GHS 150/student annually for private, GHS 50/student sponsored for public) serves as a repeating material replenishment stream, generating robust, predictable annual cash inflows with high margins as child enrollment expands.

### B. Pricing Strategy Logic
We establish two clear commercial tiers based on customer ability-to-pay and funding mechanisms:
1. **Private School Tier:**
   - **Upfront Onboarding:** **GHS 5,850** (covers 5x Rockbot sets, 5x Logic Card sets, teacher curriculum guides, and 1 term of free training, portal access, and hardware warranty).
   - **Monthly Subscription:** **GHS 50/teacher + GHS 5/student** starting Term 2 (averaging GHS 600/month per school based on 2 teachers and 100 students).
   - **Activity Books:** **GHS 150/student annually**.
   - *Validation:* Sister Beatrice (Kumasi Principal) validated that this pricing is easily absorbed by private schools by passing the cost to parents as an ICT/activity fee of GHS 50/term.
2. **Government/Sponsored Tier:**
   - **Upfront Onboarding:** **GHS 3,600 sponsored** (funded by Corporate CSR partners or development organizations like UNICEF/KOICA).
   - **Monthly Subscription:** **GHS 0** (fully sponsored/free).
   - **Activity Books:** **GHS 50/student subsidized** (funded by sponsors or subsidized at cost).

### E. #1 Revenue Risk & Mitigation Strategy
> **Revenue Risk:** "Private schools fail to collect termly activity/ICT fees from parents on time, leading to delayed subscription payments to CodesRock and causing cash flow constraints."
- **Mitigation Strategy:**
  1. **Automated Teacher Portal Access Lockouts:** Integrate automated invoice reminders in the Teacher Portal (`app.codesrock.com`). If an invoice is 30 days overdue, portal access is restricted, preventing teachers from accessing weekly lesson plans. This incentivizes prompt payment.
  2. **Flexible Billed-Termly Payment Structure:** Standard billing is aligned with the academic calendar. Private schools pay their termly GHS 1,800 subscription upfront at the beginning of the term (alongside tuition fee collections) rather than monthly, preventing intra-term school cash flow dips.

## 📋 Part 2: Competitor Revenue Model Research
We researched our direct competitors' revenue models and approximate average revenue per user (ARPU):

1. **Cubetto (by Primo Toys):**
   - **How they charge:** One-off upfront hardware e-commerce sales ($225 USD per toy playset) and discretionary expansion storybooks/maps.
   - **ARPU:** ~$250 USD (~GHS 3,650 GHS) one-time ARPU per school/customer. No recurring portal or software fees.
   - **Limitation:** High upfront pricing makes school expansion hard, with 0 GHS recurring customer value once hardware is sold.
2. **Lego Education (Spike Essential):**
   - **How they charge:** Upfront educational kit purchases (~GHS 4,500 per box, schools require at least 5-10 boxes) + annual licensing fees for software curriculums and teacher certification training.
   - **ARPU:** ~GHS 12,000 to GHS 25,000 initial purchase + GHS 2,500/year recurring software licensing.
   - **Limitation:** Exorbitant pricing and dependency on expensive computer/tablet labs, making it inaccessible to the majority of West African schools.
