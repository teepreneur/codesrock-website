### Exercise 8: MVP Development
*Defining the Minimum Viable Product needed to test our core hypothesis.*

**The Core Hypothesis:** We can teach coding effectively without screens.
**The MVP:** 
1. **The Physical Kit:** 1 functional Rockbot, 1 Starter deck of Logic Cards, 1 mapped physical mat.
2. **The Content:** 1 printed chapter of the Ghanaian-themed Activity Book and 1 unplugged group game framework.
3. **The Deployment:** A 2-hour guided beta-test session with a pilot school or parent focus group, where we manually act as the "Teacher Portal" guiding the adults.
*We do not need a fully automated web portal or 100 character songs to validate the MVP. We just need the physical loop (Card → Robot → Movement).*

### Exercise 9: "To Be" Scenario Map
*Mapping "Conscious Akosua's" new experience now that her school has adopted CodesRock.*

*   **1. The Trigger:** Akosua picks up her child from school.
*   **2. The New Action:** Her child excitedly explains how they built an "algorithm" with their friends using cards and a robot.
*   **3. The Thinking:** Akosua thinks, "My child is learning advanced logic, interacting socially, and there wasn't a screen in sight."
*   **4. The Feeling:** Relief, immense pride, vindication that her advocacy worked.
*   **5. The Loyalty Loop:** Akosua posts a photo of the CodesRock activity book on Instagram, organically marketing the B2C Home Kit to other parents and validating the school's decision.

### Exercise 10: Experience-Based Roadmap & Product Feature List
*How the product rolls out over time based on user feedback.*

**Phase 1: The Tactical MVP (Current/Near-term)**
*   Core Rockbot with basic directional sensing.
*   Standard Logic Card Deck (Forward, Turn, Loop).
*   Physical Activity Book 1 (Basic Logic).

**Phase 2: The Institutional Scale (Mid-term)**
*   Launch of Web-based Teacher Portal (Lesson plans & tracking).
*   Classroom Multi-Packs (Bulk kits optimized for groups of 4 students).
*   Original Audio/Songs library released to schools.

**Phase 3: The Expanding Universe (Long-term)**
*   Advanced sensors for Rockbot (color detection, obstacle avoidance).
*   Advanced Card Expansion Packs (If/Then, Variables).
*   "Levels" of Activity Books increasing in complexity.

## 🎙️ Friday, April 17 (Day 5) 
### "Pitch Friday" Script (3 Minutes)

**The Hook (15 seconds)**

"65% of children entering primary school today will end up in jobs that don't even exist yet. The common language in all of those jobs? Code. But how do we teach coding to a 5-year-old without gluing them to a screen and damaging their social and motor development?"

**The Problem (30 seconds)**
"Currently, schools face two massive hurdles: building computer labs is incredibly expensive, and parents are increasingly hostile toward screen-time. This leaves a massive gap in early childhood STEM education across Africa."

**The Solution (45 seconds)**
"Enter CodesRock. We provide a completely screen-free coding ecosystem. We use tactile Logic Cards to program our physical interactive robot, Rockbot. We pair this with culturally relevant Ghanaian activity books, unplugged physical games, and original music. It’s collaborative, it gets kids moving, and it requires zero screens."

**The Business Model & Traction (45 seconds)**
"Our secret weapon is our B2B2C model. We sell directly to parents who want to future-proof their kids. But more importantly, we empower these engaged parents to act as advocates, introducing our curriculum to their schools. We then sell Institutional Kits and annual Teacher Portal subscriptions to the schools. Our MVP is built, our curriculum is mapped, and parents like our persona 'Akosua' are already proving our demand hypothesis."

**The Ask (30 seconds)**
"Through the UNICEF StartUp Lab, we are looking for strategic partnerships and early-stage capital to manufacture our first bulk run of Classroom Multi-Packs and deploy into our first 10 pilot schools. Let's make coding tactile, social, and screen-free. Thank you."
