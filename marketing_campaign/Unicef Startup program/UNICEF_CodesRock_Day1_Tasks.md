# UNICEF StartUp Lab 6 – Product Module: Day 1 Preparation
## Prototyping & UI/UX Foundations (CodesRock)

---

### Session 1: Prototyping - Turning Ideas into Tangible Concepts (9:00 AM)
**Objective:** Move from "Screen-Free Coding" as a concept to a physical UI/UX specification.

**Tangible Concepts Developed:**
*   **The Logic Card Specification:** Defined standard physical dimensions (63.5mm x 88.9mm) and matte finishing for optimal sensor reading.
*   **Unique Color-Coded Interface:** 
    *   **Forward (Orange), Left (Blue), Right (Yellow), Loop (Pink).**
    *   **Hardware Sync:** Colors mirror the physical buttons on the Rockbot to simplify the "Visual Programming" experience for children.
*   **Cultural Integration:** Embedded Kente geometric patterns into the card borders to ensure the product feels local and authentic to the Ghanaian market.

---
 
### Session 2: Building Realistic Prototypes (11:00 AM)
**Objective:** Generate high-fidelity visual representations of the final product.
 
**Prototypes Created:**
1.  **Logic Cards (Core Deck):** High-contrast, color-coded cards (Orange, Blue, Yellow, Pink) with Kente border accents.
2.  **Rockbot Hardware Prototype:** A friendly, tactile robot with glowing "status" antennae and a top-panel "Command Cluster" of buttons mirroring the card colors.
 
*These prototypes demonstrate a cohesive ecosystem where hardware and software (cards) share a unified visual language.*

---

### Session 3: Understanding UI vs UX (2:00 PM)
**Objective:** Define the user-centered design principles for an unplugged product.

**UI (User Interface):**
*   High-contrast white icons on dark vibrant backgrounds.
*   The "Rocky Seal" as a watermark of quality and brand recognition.
*   Tactile cardstock (350gsm) for durability in active classroom environments.

**UX (User Experience):**
*   **Feedback Loop:** The child places a card -> The Rockbot scans/reads -> The physical motion executes.
*   **Error Handling:** If a sequence is logically incorrect, the Rockbot provides audio/visual cues (to be defined in Phase 2: Mobile/Robot UX).
*   **Collaboration:** The size and physical nature of the cards encourage group discussion and "collective debugging."
