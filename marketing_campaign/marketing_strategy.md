# CodesRock Marketing Campaign Strategy: "Code Without Screens"

## 1. Executive Summary
This campaign aims to position CodesRock as the premier solution for STEAM education in Ghana (and Africa), specifically solving the "lack of infrastructure" problem by offering **screen-free** computational thinking tools. The strategy uses a "Pincer Movement":
1.  **Top-Down**: Directly pitching schools on the competitive advantage of offering STEM.
2.  **Bottom-Up**: Mobilizing parents to demand modern skills for their children, creating pressure on schools to adopt the curriculum.

**Campaign Timeline:** 4 Weeks (Launch -> High Frequency -> Sustain)

## 2. Target Audiences

| Segment | Persona | Pain Points | Motivations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Schools (Primary)** | School Owners, Principals, Head Teachers | • High cost of computer labs<br>• Need to differentiate from competitors<br>• Teacher training burden | • Attract more premium students<br>• Marketing prestige ("We are a STEM school")<br>• Cost-effective implementation |
| **Parents (Secondary)** | Middle-class parents, 25-45, Urban/Peri-urban | • Worry about child's future job security<br>• Fear of "too much screen time"<br>• Want affordable quality education | • Child's intelligence/success<br>• "Future-proofing" their kids<br>• Fun learning (screen-free) |

## 3. Core Messaging Pillars
*   **For Schools**: "Turn any classroom into a cutting-edge STEM lab for a fraction of the cost."
*   **For Parents**: "Your child can learn to code *before* they even touch a computer. Give them the head start they deserve."
*   **The "Hook"**: Screen-Free. (Solves the health concern for parents and the cost concern for schools).

## 4. Phase 1: School Acquisition (The "Prestige" Angle)
**Goal:** Secure institutional partnerships.
**Channels:** LinkedIn, Direct Email, WhatsApp (Direct).

*   **Strategy**: Position CodesRock not as a "toy", but as a **Curriculum Upgrade**.
*   **Offer**: "School Partnership Program" – Full Training, Curriculum, and Hardware.
*   **Key Asset**: The "School Value Proposal" (focused on ROI for the school).

## 5. Phase 2: Parent Advocacy (The "Demand" Angle)
**Goal:** Parents buy Home Kits OR ask their school to buy in.
**Channels:** Facebook (Groups), Instagram/TikTok (Visuals), WhatsApp (Community).

*   **Strategy**: "The Grassroots Push". Create "FOMO" (Fear Of Missing Out). Show other kids excelling with CodesRock.
*   **Call to Action (CTA)**:
    1.  "Buy the Home Kit" (Immediate Revenue).
    2.  "Tell your School Principal you want CodesRock" (Long-term Contract).

## 6. Channel Execution Plan

### LinkedIn (B2B - Schools)
*   **Focus**: Professionalism, Educational Outcomes, Cost Savings.
*   **Content**: Success stories of partner schools, "Future of Work" articles, Stats on STEM demand.

### Facebook & Instagram (B2C - Parents)
*   **Focus**: Fun, Child Development, Pride.
*   **Content**: High-energy videos of kids playing with the robot, Testimonials from proud parents ("My 5-year-old is coding!").

### TikTok (Virality)
*   **Focus**: The "Magic" Moment.
*   **Content**: 15-second clips of the robot following commands. "Did you know you can code without a screen?"

### WhatsApp (Conversion)
*   **Focus**: Direct connection, Trust.
*   **Content**: PDF Brochures for parents to read, Status updates with results/photos.
